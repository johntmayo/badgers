The 989th episode and the season premiere of [[Season 51]] of {{snl}} premiered on October 4, 2025, hosted by Puerto Rican rapper, singer-songwriter, record producer and actor [[Bad Bunny]], with musical guest [[Doja Cat]]. This is Bad Bunny's 2nd time as the host and his 5th appearance, and Doja Cat made her SNL musical guest debut.

Bad Bunny previously made three musical guest appearances and did double duty on SNL on the following dates: On [[February 20, 2021]], the thirteenth episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 46|46th season]], hosted by British actor [[Regé-Jean Page]], on [[October 21, 2023]], the second episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 49|49th season]], when he hosted for the first time and he also made his 2nd musical guest appearance, as well as his 3rd appearance on SNL in total, and on [[May 17, 2025]], which is the [[Season 50]] finale, hosted by actress [[Scarlett Johansson]].

This is the first episode for new cast members [[Tommy Brennan]], [[Jeremy Culhane]], [[Ben Marshall]], [[Kam Patterson]], and [[Veronika Slowikowska]].

The goodnight speech for Bad Bunny is "Thank You Everybody, (Cheers and applause), Thank You Doja Cat, Jon Hamm, Benicio del Toro, HUNTR/X and My Guy Petey, That's Has Been Here 51 Years. (Speaking Spanish) Gracias por tanto, Los quiero. Los amo. Los quero. Thank you Lorne, I Love You". (Cheers and applause again)

==Trivia==
*Outside of the show's strike-delayed return in 2023, this marked the latest fall premiere for ''SNL'' since 2003, when [[Season 29]] also kicked off on [[October 4, 2003]].
*Prior to joining the cast, [[Ben Marshall]] had been a writer for four seasons, as part of his comedy troupe [[Please Don't Destroy]].
**However, the group will no longer be contributing shorts to the show, due to writer/member [[John Higgins]] leaving the show prior to this season, but fellow writer/member [[Martin Herlihy]] will remain on the show, as just a writer.
*This also marked the first episode for new writers: [[Jack Bensinger]], [[Tucker Flodman]], [[Maxwell Gay]], [[Claire McFadden]], [[Rachel Pegram]], [[Jo Sunday]], and [[Maddie Wiener]].
*Starting with this episode, [[Chloe Fineman]] (who has been a cast member since [[September 28, 2019|2019]]) becomes the longest-tenured ''current'' female cast member, as she enters her seventh season in the cast.
*Additionally, starting with this episode, [[Erik Kenward]] (a longtime writer/producer for the show since [[February 24, 2001|2001]]) has been promoted to a head writer.
*Also, [[Erin Doyle]] (a longtime producer for the show since [[September 26, 2009|2009]]) has been promoted to a main producer for the show, alongside Kenward and [[Steve Higgins]].
*Longtime writer [[Bryan Tucker]] is on a temporary leave, for the first-half of the season.
*Lastly, [[Ken Aymong]] (a former longtime writing supervisor who worked there from [[September 25, 1982|1982]] to [[May 22, 2021|2021]]), is back in his own role.

The 990th episode and 2nd episode of {{snl}}<nowiki/>'''s'' [[Season 51|51st season]] premiered on October 11, 2025. Premiering on the [[October 11, 1975|50th anniversary]] of the show's premiere, hosted by longtime former 2000s-era cast member and ''[[Weekend Update]]'' co-anchor [[Amy Poehler]], the host of the popular podcast ''Good Hang with Amy Poehler'' on Spotify, and the star of the NBC's classic hit sitcom ''Parks & Recreation'', as well as the hit films ''Wet Hot American Summer'', ''Mean Girls'', ''Envy'', ''Man of the Year'', ''The Ex'', ''Blades of Glory'', ''On Broadway'', ''Mr. Woodcock'', ''Baby Mama'', ''Hamlet 2'', ''Spring Breakdown'', ''Freak Dance'', ''A.C.O.D.'', ''Are You Here'', ''They Came Together'', ''A Very Murray Christmas'', ''Sisters'', ''The House'', ''Wine Country'', ''Moxie'' and ''First Time Female Director'', the live-action ''Alvin and the Chipmunks'' film series, and the hit animated films ''Shrek the Third'', ''Horton Hears a Who!'', ''Monsters vs. Aliens'', ''Free Birds'' and Disney's hit animated films ''Inside Out'' and the sequel ''Inside Out 2'', with musical guest [[Role Model]], an singer-songwriter and former rapper. This is Amy Poehler's 2nd individual SNL hosting appearance and her 3rd time hosting overall, as well as her first solo hosting appearance in 15 years, 6 months, and 19 days. This is also the musical guest debut on SNL for Role Model.

Poehler previously hosted SNL on the following dates: On [[September 25, 2010]], which is the [[Season 36]] premiere, with musical guest [[Katy Perry]], and on [[December 19, 2015]], the ninth episode and Christmas show of ''SNL's'' [[Season 41|41st season]], co-hosting alongside her longtime friend/former castmate [[Tina Fey]], with musical guests [[Bruce Springsteen]] and [[The E-Street Band]].

The goodnight speech for Amy Poehler is "Thank you to Role Model, (Cheers and applause), Tina Fey, Seth Myers, Charli XCX. Thanks everybody. Happy Anniversary to my parents, and happy birthday Greg, I love you all. Thanks so much for having me back again, its always a dream of a lifetime to be here, Thank you, Goodnight!". (Cheers and applause again).

The 991st episode and 3rd episode of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'s'' [[Season 51|51st season]] premiered on October 18, 2025. Pop sensation, singer, songwriter and actress [[Sabrina Carpenter]] pull her double duty as both the host and musical guest. She first served made her musical guest debut on [[May 18, 2024]] for the [[Season 49]] finale, hosted by actor [[Jake Gyllenhaal]]; which coincidentally, was the final episode for former cast members: [[Punkie Johnson]], [[Molly Kearney]] and [[Chloe Troast]], and later served as one of the musical guests for the [[Saturday Night Live 50th Anniversary Special|50th Anniversary Special]]. This is Sabrina Carpenter's first-time hosting and she made her 2nd musical guest appearance on SNL.

The goodnight speech for Sabrina Carpenter is "And That's Our Show, Thank you everybody, Thank you so much to Lorne, Thank you to the Amazing Cast and Crew, Um.. This has been one of my biggest dreams and I'm so, so grateful, Thank you guys for having me, Thank you for laughing, and Thank you for being such an amazing audience, and I hope you have a great night, tonight". (Cheers and applause)

*Sabrina Carpenter joined the famous group of SNL hosts who have performed "double duty" on the show (headlined as the host & musical guest).
*Sabrina Carpenter also joined the list of Disney stars who have hosted SNL.

**During "Nobody's Son", Carpenter swore twice, which aired uncensored on the East Coast live broadcast. It was censored in the form of audio dropouts for the West Coast broadcast, post-broadcast distribution online, as well as in airings internationally.

The 992nd episode and 4th episode of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'s'' [[Season 51|51st season]] premiered on November 1, 2025, hosted by actor [[Miles Teller]], star of the hit films ''Whiplash'', ''Thank You for Your Service'', ''The Spectacular Now,'' the ''Divergent'' film trilogy, ''21 & Over'', ''That Awkward Moment'', ''Fantastic Four'', ''The Ark and the Aardvark'', ''Spiderhead'', ''Two Night Stand'', ''Get a Job'', ''War Dogs'', ''Bleed for This'', ''Top Gun: Maverick'' and ''Eternity'', the Amazon Prime Video crime drama ''Too Old to Die Young'' and the Paramount+ miniseries ''The Offer'', with singer-songwriter, producer and musician [[Brandi Carlile]] as the musical guest. Teller previously hosted SNL on [[October 1, 2022]], which is the [[Season 48]] premiere, with musical guest [[Kendrick Lamar]]; which coincidentally, was the first episode for current cast member [[Marcello Hernandez]]. This is Miles Teller's 2nd time hosting and Brandi Carlile's 3rd individual solo musical guest appearance on SNL, as well as her 4th overall musical guest appearance.

Brandi Carlile previously made her 3rd musical guest appearances on SNL on the following dates: On [[October 23, 2021]], the fourth episode and Halloween-themed show of ''SNL's'' [[Season 47|47th season]], hosted by longtime former cast member [[Jason Sudeikis]], on [[December 10, 2022]], the eighth and Holidays-themed episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 48|48th season]], hosted by actor, comedian, writer, composer, playwright, producer, and musician [[Steve Martin]] and former short-term Ebersol-era (and former ''SCTV'') cast member [[Martin Short]]; together they stars of the Hulu's hit mystery comedy-drama series ''Only Murders in the Building'', and on [[April 5, 2025]], the sixteenth episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 50|50th season]], hosted by comedian, actor and musician [[Jack Black]] (which he returned to host SNL for the 4th time and his first appearance in 20 years, 3 months, and 19 days), along with [[Elton John]].

The goodnight speech for Miles Teller is "Thanks to Brandi Carlile, Ramy Youssef, Shane Gillis, Lorne, The Band, The Cast. Thank You all for joining us, I had a lot of fun this week, so thank you all. Thank you. Goodnight, goodnight". (Cheers and applause)

The 995th episode and 7th episode of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'s'' [[Season 51|51st season]] premiered on December 6, 2025, hosted by actress, comedian, screenwriter and producer [[Melissa McCarthy]], star of the CBS' classic hit sitcom ''Mike & Molly'' and Paramount+'s new true crime drama limited series ''Unspeakable: The Murder of JonBenét Ramsey'', as well as the star of the hit films ''Cook Off!'', ''The Nines'', ''Pretty Ugly People'', ''Life as We Know It'', ''Bridesmaids'', ''This is 40'', ''The Hangover Part III'', ''Identity Thief'', ''The Heat'', ''Tammy'', ''St. Vincent'', ''Spy'', ''The Boss'', ''Life of the Party'', ''The Happytime Murders'', ''Can You Ever Forgive Me?'', ''The Kitchen'', ''Superintelligence'', ''Thunder Force'', ''The Starling'', ''Genie'' and ''Unfrosted'', and Disney's hit live-action adaptation musical romantic fantasy film ''The Little Mermaid'', with musical guest [[Dijon]], an R&B singer-songwriter, record producer and multi-instrumentalist. This is Melissa McCarthy's 6th time hosting SNL (the first time in 8 years), thus making her the fourth woman to hosting the show more than five and tied with [[Drew Barrymore]], [[Tina Fey]] and [[Scarlett Johansson]] as the most frequent female ''SNL'' host in the show's history. This is also the musical guest debut that Dijon made on SNL.

Melissa McCarthy previously hosted SNL on the following dates: On [[October 1, 2011]], the 2nd episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 37|37th season]], with musical guests [[Lady Antebellum|Lady A]], an country music group, on [[April 6, 2013]], the 17th episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 38|38th season]], with musical guests [[Phoenix]], a legendary French alternative rock band, on [[February 1, 2014]], the 13th episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 39|39th season]], with musical guests [[Imagine Dragons]], an legendary pop rock band; which coincidentally, was the final episode for longtime former cast member, head writer and ''[[Weekend Update]]'' anchor [[Seth Meyers]], on [[February 13, 2016]], the 13th episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 41|41st season]], with musical guest [[Kanye West]], and on [[May 13, 2017]], the 20th/penultimate episode, as well as the Mother's Day show of ''SNL's'' [[Season 42|42nd season]], with musical guests [[HAIM]], (which she returned to host SNL for the 5th time, thus making her the then-fifth female five-time host and becomes the then-newest member to enter the [[Five-Timers Club]]). 

The goodnight speech for Melissa McCarthy is "Thank You to the Amazing Dijon and his band, Thank you SNL, Thank you New York City, I Love You Ben and Georgie and Vivi, All of My Friends who Came Here Tonight, Happy Holidays, Everyone, Woo!". (Cheers and applause)

The 996th episode and 8th (and Holidays-themed) episode of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'s'' [[Season 51|51st season]] premiered on December 13, 2025, hosted by British actor [[Josh O'Connor]], star of the Netflix's hit historical drama series ''The Crown'' and ITV's classic hit British comedy-drama series ''The Durrells'' and the hit films ''The Magnificent Eleven'', ''Hide and Seek'', ''The Riot Club'', ''Bridgend'',  Disney's live-action adaptation romantic fantasy film ''Cinderella'', ''The Program'', ''Florence Foster Jenkins'', ''God's Own Country'', ''Only You'', ''Hope Gap'', ''Emma'', ''Mothering Sunday'', ''Aisha'', ''La chimera'',  ''Lee'', ''Bonus Track'', ''Challengers'', ''Rebuilding'', ''The History of Sound'', ''The Mastermind'' and ''Wake Up Dead Man: A Knives Out Mystery'', with musical guest [[Lily Allen]], who performed "Sleepwalking" and "Madeline" and appears in the "Lily Allen Brunch" sketch. 

Lily Allen previously made her musical guest debut on [[February 3, 2007]], the 12th episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 32|32nd season]], hosted by actress [[Drew Barrymore]] (when she returned to host SNL for the 5th time, thus making her the then-second female five-time host to enter the [[Five-Timers Club]]).

This was Josh O'Connor's "SNL" hosting debut, as well as Lily Allen made her 2nd musical guest appearance and her first appearance in 18 years, 10 months and 10 days on SNL.

The goodnight speech for Josh O'Connor is "Big My Thanks to Lily Allen and Dakota Johnson, (Cheers and applause), Thank you very much and Good night, Thank you". (Cheers and applause again)

{{Episode Infobox|season=[[Season 51]]|episodenumber=997|seasonnumber=9|host=[[Ariana Grande]]|musicalguest=[[Cher]]|musicalguestsongs="'''DJ Play a Christmas Song'''" <br> "'''Run Rudolph Run'''"|previousepisode=[[December 13, 2025]]|nextepisode=[[January 17, 2026]]|imagehost=[[File:ArianaGrande51.png|300px]]|imagemusicalguest=[[File:Cher51.png|300px]]}}

The 997th episode and 9th episode, as well as the Christmas episode of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'s'' [[Season 51|51st season]] premiered on December 20, 2025, hosted by pop sensation, singer-songwriter and actress [[Ariana Grande]], the former star of Nickelodeon's hit television shows ''Victorious'' (2010-2013) and spin-off ''Sam & Cat'' (2013-2014) and the star of the hit musical fantasy film ''Wicked'' (2024) and the sequel ''Wicked: For Good'' (2025), with musical guest [[Cher]], an singer-songwriter and the "Goddess of Pop", who performed "DJ Play a Christmas Song" and "Run Rudolph Run" with Captain Kirk Douglas of [[The Roots]] and the [[Saturday Night Live Band]] and appears in Bowen's farewell sketch, "Delta Lounge".

Ariana Grande previously made her 3rd musical guest appearances and hosted two times on SNL on the following dates: On [[September 27, 2014]], which is the season premiere of [[Season 40]] of ''Saturday Night Live'', hosted by actor [[Chris Pratt]]; this marked the first episode for current cast member and ''[[Weekend Update]]'' co-anchor [[Michael Che]], on [[March 12, 2016]], the 15th episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 41|41st season]], when she hosted for the first time and also served as the episode's musical guest, on [[March 9, 2024]], the 14th episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 49|49th season]], hosted by actor [[Josh Brolin]] (when he returned to host SNL for the 3rd time and his first appearance in 12 years, 11 months and 7 days), and on [[October 12, 2024]], the 3rd episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 50|50th anniversary season]], with musical guest [[Stevie Nicks]].

Cher previously made her musical guest debut on [[November 21, 1987]], the 5th episode and Thanksgiving-themed show of ''SNL's'' [[Season 13|13th season]], hosted by actress [[Candice Bergen]] (when she returned to host SNL for the 4th time and her first appearance in over a decade).

This is Ariana Grande's 3rd time hosting SNL, and her 5th appearance, thus marking her fifth appearance on SNL, three times as host and three times as the musical guest. This is also the 2nd musical guest appearance for Cher and her first appearance in 38 years, 1 month, and 29 days on SNL.

This marked the final episode for longtime cast member [[Bowen Yang]] (started on the writing staff in [[Season 44|2018]], and he had been a cast member since [[September 28, 2019|2019]]), as he left the show after seven years following the Christmas episode. He would be the sixth cast member to leave the show in 2025, following the former cast members listed above.

The goodnight speech for Ariana Grande is "Many Thanks to Cher, Aidy Bryant, and Bowen Yang. We love you so much! (Cheers and applause), We love you so much, good night! Thank you, New York!". (Cheers and applause again)

The 998th episode and 10th episode, as well as the first show of New Year 2026 of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'s'' [[Season 51|51st season]] premiere on January 17, 2026, hosted by Canadian actor, musician, and film director [[Finn Wolfhard]], star of the Netflix's hit science fiction horror drama series ''Stranger Things'' and the hit films ''It'' and its sequel ''It Chapter Two'', the supernatural comedy film ''Ghostbusters: Afterlife'' and its sequel ''Ghostbusters: Frozen Empire'', ''The Goldfinch'', ''The Turning'', ''When You Finish Saving the World'', ''Hell of a Summer'', ''Saturday Night'' and ''The Legend of Ochi'', and the hit animated films ''Howard Lovecraft and the Kingdom of Madness'', ''The Addams Family'' and ''Guillermo del Toro's Pinocchio'' with musical guest [[A$AP Rocky]], who performed "PUNK ROCKY" and a medley of "DON'T BE DUMB"/"TRIP BABY" and "HELICOPTER" and appears in the "Snack Homiez" sketch.

This was Finn Wolfhard's hosting debut, thus making him the sixth cast member from ''Stranger Things'' to host ''SNL'', as well as the third cast member from ''It'' and the fifth person from Jason Reitman's ''Saturday Night'' to host. This is also the 2nd appearance and the musical guest debut that A$AP Rocky made on SNL.

The goodnight speech for Finn Wolfhard is "Hey, My Thanks to A$AP Rocky, (Cheers and applause), Caleb McLaughlin, Gaten Matarazzo, Sabrina Carpenter, This Has Been... This Has Been One of the Best Week of My Life and One of the Best Cast Ever, This is a Dream Come True, Thank you all so much, Love you all, Thank you". (Cheers and applause again)

==Trivia==
*This marked the first SNL episode of 2026.
*A New Year, another new host. January is the month with the most first-time hosts in SNL history.
*SNL Headliners Finn Wolfhard & A$AP Rocky are the 31st pair to kick off a New Year as the first-time Host and Musical Guest.
*Finn Wolfhard is the 6th cast member from ''Stranger Things'' to host ''SNL'', after [[Matthew Modine]], [[Linda Hamilton]], [[Paul Reiser]], [[Winona Ryder]] and [[David Harbour]], as well as the third cast member from ''It'' to host, after [[James McAvoy]] and [[Jessica Chastain]].
**Finn Wolfhard is the first member of the younger cast- the "''Stranger Things'' kids" -to host ''Saturday Night Live''.
**Finn Wolfhard is also the fifth person from Jason Reitman's ''Saturday Night'' to appear on SNL.
**Finn Wolfhard is the latest SNL connection to Adult Swim show Smiling Friends.
**Finn Wolfhard is the tenth actor from the ''Ghostbusters'' franchise to host, not counting [[Leslie Jones]] who was a longrunning castmember, but is yet to host.
*Finn Wolfhard ends the second-longest streak of episodes in Saturday Night Live history without a host under 25. He is one of the first SNL hosts born in the 2000s.
**There is a youth movement happening with Saturday Night Live hosts this season.
*Before the show, the SNL audience was treated to a new warmup song: "Let's Go Crazy" by [[Prince]], as sung by [[Tommy Brennan]] and [[Veronika Slowikowska]].
**The new warmup replaces longtime warmup song "Gimme Some Lovin", as sung by [[Kenan Thompson]] before each show since [[Season 38]].
*Beginning with this episode, [[Jeremy Culhane]] takes over playing [[JD Vance]] from [[Bowen Yang]] and [[Ashley Padilla]] takes over playing [[Kristi Noem]] from [[Tina Fey]].
*[[James Austin Johnson]] says [["Live from New York, it's Saturday Night"]] for the 46th time, moves into a tie for 5th place all-time with [[Alec Baldwin]] and [[Colin Jost]] says "Live from New York, it's Saturday Night" for the 12th time, moves into a tie for 43rd place of all-time.
*[[Marcello Hernandez]] reached his 200th milestone sketch on ''SNL''.
*Before the goodnights, a still photo of Bob Weir, who died on January 10, is shown in silence.

The 12th episode of [[Season 51]] and the 1,000th milestone episode of ''[[Saturday Night Live]]'' premiered on January 31, 2026. It was hosted by Swedish Emmy and Golden Globe Award-winning actor [[Alexander Skarsgård]], star of the hit films ''Zoolander'', ''The Dog Trick'', ''Om Sara'', ''The Last Drop'', ''Never Be Mine'', ''Kill Your Darlings'', ''Score'', ''Exit'', ''Järnets änglar'', ''Beyond the Pole'', ''13'', ''Straw Dogs'', ''Melancholia'', ''Battleship'', ''What Maisie Knew'', ''Disconnect'', ''The East'', ''The Giver'', ''Hidden'', ''The Diary of a Teenage Girl'', ''War on Everyone'', ''The Legend of Tarzan'', ''Mute'', ''The Hummingbird Project'', ''Hold the Dark'', ''The Aftermath'', ''Long Shot'', ''The Kill Team'', ''Passing'', ''Godzilla vs. Kong'', ''The Northman'', ''Infinity Pool'', ''Eric Larue'', ''Lee'', ''The Moment'' and ''Pillon'', with rapper [[Cardi B]] as the musical guest, who performed "Bodega Baddie" with El Prodigio and "ErrTime" and appeared in the "Immigrant Dad Talk Show" sketch. This was Alexander Skarsgård's hosting debut. This is also Cardi B's second musical guest appearance and her first appearance on SNL in nearly eight years. 

Cardi B previously made her musical guest debut on [[April 7, 2018]], the seventeenth episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 43|43rd season]], hosted by late actor, writer, director, and playwright [[Chadwick Boseman]].

Alexander Skarsgård's goodbye speech is "My thanks to Cardi B (cheers and applause), Pete Davidson, Stellan Skarsgård, my Uncle Jack McBrayer, Lorne Michaels, [and the] cast and crew. It's been an incredible week. Thank you so much. Thank you everyone for coming out." (Cheers and applause again)

Henson]], and on [[December 8, 2018]], the eighth and Holidays-themed episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 44|44th season]], hosted by actor [[Jason Momoa]].

Movie'' and the sequel ''The Super Mario Galaxy Movie'', with musical guest [[Jack White]], an musician, singer and songwriter. Jack Black returned to hosting SNL for the 5th time and becomes the newest member of the [[Five-Timers Club]]. 

This is Jack Black's 5th time hosting SNL, thus making him a five-time host, and he also becomes the newest member of the [[Five-Timers Club]] and Jack White made his 6th musical guest stint, as well as his 5th solo musical guest appearance on SNL. 

Black previously hosted ''SNL'' 4 times on the following dates: On [[January 19, 2002]], the eleventh episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 27|27th season]], with musical guests [[The Strokes]], on [[October 4, 2003]], which is the season premiere of [[Season 29]], with musical guest [[John Mayer]]; this marked the first episode for longest-running cast member [[Kenan Thompson]], on [[December 17, 2005]], the ninth episode and Christmas show of ''SNL's'' [[Season 31|31st season]], with musical guest [[Neil Young]], and on [[April 5, 2025]], the sixteenth episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 50|50th anniversary season]] (when he returned to host SNL for the 4th time and his first appearance in 20 years, 3 months, and 19 days), with musical guests [[Elton John]] and [[Brandi Carlile]]. 

Black also made his special guest appearances with [[Tenacious D]] on SNL on the following dates: On [[May 2, 1998]], the nineteenth (and penultimate) episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 23|23rd season]], hosted by actor [[Matthew Broderick]], with musical guest [[Natalie Merchant]] (as they made their special guest only), and they were also the musical guest on [[December 2, 2006]], the seventh episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 32|32nd season]], hosted by actor [[Matthew Fox]]. 

Jack White previously made his made first appearance as the musical guest on SNL when he was on the band [[The White Stripes]] for their only musical guest appearance, as well as his musical guest debut on [[October 19, 2002]], the third episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 28|28th season]], hosted by the politician and United States Navy officer [[John McCain]].

He also previously made his 5th musical guest stints and his 4th solo musical guest appearances on SNL on the following dates: On [[March 3, 2012]], the sixteenth episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 37|37th season]], hosted by actress [[Lindsay Lohan]], on [[April 14, 2018]], the eighteenth episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 43|43rd season]], hosted by former ''SNL'' writer,  stand-up comedian and actor [[John Mulaney]], on [[October 10, 2020]], the second episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 46|46th season]], hosted by actor, stand-up comedian, voice actor and podcaster [[Bill Burr]], and on [[February 25, 2023]], the thirteenth episode of ''SNL's'' [[Season 48|48th season]], hosted by actor [[Woody Harrelson]], which they making both of them five-timers to enter the [[Five-Timers Club]].

The goodnight speech for Jack Black is "Thanks to Jack White, Jonah Hill, Tina Fey, Candice Bergen, Melissa McCarthy, this is the best show in the world, thank you Lorne, this cast is truly the best, I love you". (Cheers and applause)
